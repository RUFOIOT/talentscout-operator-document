Build Time

The AI Operator Cohort is a single, focused program designed to take marketing professionals from passive AI users to confident system builders — in four weeks. The curriculum is intentionally scoped for first cohorts: one program, one audience, one clear outcome.

Design principle: Keep it simple for the first cohorts until we figure out what new classes might look like. One program. Learn from it. Evolve from there.

The Problem This Solves

Most professionals are still copy-pasting into ChatGPT — no context, no system, no structure. Every session starts blank. Output is generic. Nothing compounds. Teams pulling ahead aren't replacing people — they're building fluent operators. The AI isn't the differentiator. The co-work skill is.

The Outcome

After 4 weeks, participants go from reactive AI users to system builders. Their AI works while they sleep, remembers their brand, and ships work they used to outsource. They leave with a deployed stack, a live HTML artifact, and a certificate — not a certificate of attendance, but proof they built something real.

Teaching Philosophy

85%+ build time. Every session produces a working artifact. No theory without immediate application. The cohort inherits a pre-tested stack — every tool, template, and automation has been validated before session one. Participants build on what works, not what might work.

Each session also includes a short pre-session theory video (15–20 min) delivered before the live session, so live time is protected for building. Weekly office hours (last 30 min of Session 2 and 4 each week) give participants unstructured build support.

The 6-Layer Operator Stack

The curriculum assembles a complete 6-layer stack over 4 weeks. Each layer builds on the previous, so participants arrive at Week 4 with a fully integrated system to assemble — not a collection of disconnected tools.

  1. Workspaces — Specialized Claude Co-Work environments by function (Research, Content, Operations)
  2. Agents — Configured AI team members with roles, context, and recurring tasks
  3. Automations — Scheduled and triggered workflows that run without manual input
  4. Knowledge Bases — Brand, client, and performance data loaded and verified on-brand
  5. MCP Connectors — Live connections to Slack, Notion, Figma, Higgsfield, and Google Workspace
  6. HTML Artifacts — Deployable front-end reports, dashboards, and briefs built with Claude

05
Show & Tell — Week 4 Demo

Every cohort ends with a live public demo event in Week 4. It's not a graduation ceremony — it's a structured sales event disguised as a showcase. Open to clients, prospects, and anyone curious about what AI operators can build.

The 3-Minute Demo Format

0:00 – 1:00
What You Built
Open Claude Co-Work live. Show workspaces, knowledge bases, and connected tools. Name the agent. One-sentence stack map. Open the live HTML artifact URL.
1:00 – 2:00
Live Demo
Run the agent or automation in real time — real input, real output. Let it execute. Silence is powerful. The audience watches it happen.
2:00 – 3:00
The Number That Matters
State the exact before/after. Name the task and the time it used to take. Show side-by-side. Close with: "This is just one piece of my stack."

"A live demo that stumbles and recovers is more powerful than a perfect screenshot. Mastery of the system — not perfection of the output."
